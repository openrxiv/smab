## openRxiv Scientific and Medical Advisory Board Meeting Minutes
8 April 2026

### Background
openRxiv’s Scientific and Medical Advisory Board (SMAB) is an important, community-representative group of scientists who advise the management team on content and policy issues that arise around bioRxiv and medRxiv and help the team develop openRxiv’s strategy as a mission-driven, not-for-profit organization. The 15 current SMAB members are listed in the leadership section of the openRxiv website. They have varied scientific experience and specialties, and are based in seven different countries. 

The inaugural SMAB meetings were held by Zoom on April 8, 2026 at 9.00am and 7pm Eastern time, so that members could choose the more convenient time. Both lasted 1 hour and had the same formal agenda, with time available for questions and discussion.

**Agenda**
* Welcome and introductions (John Inglis) (10 min)
* openRxiv’s operations and progress (Richard Sever and Tracy Teal) (10 min)
* About the SMAB and Working Groups (John Inglis) (10 min)
* Discussion: Scientific Integrity and the Challenge of AI (All) (25 min)
* Wrap-up and next steps (John Inglis) (5 min)

### Summary
Tracy and Richard provided a brief overview of:
* the transition of bioRxiv and medRxiv and associated staff to the new 501(c)(3) openRxiv for long-term stewardship of the servers
openRxiv’s organizational structure
* the beginnings of the development of key partnerships with other organizations such as Hypothesis (to build a new commenting platform), Dryad (permitting preprint authors to obtain data curation and storage services), and qed (permitting authors to obtain AI-based manuscript assessment before or after posting a preprint)
* technical improvements such as the opportunity for authors to name their funder in the preprint submission process
* an emerging vision of preprints as a central component in a connected ecosystem (a constellation) of research outputs that include data, code, and assessments/reviews
* the aspiration that openRxiv can lead in the development of a more efficient and equitable scholarly communication system

John briefly described:
* the aspirations for the work of the SMAB, with reference to a proposed framework for the responsibilities of members described here https://gogl.to/462X.
* the hope that members would agree to be called on from time to time for advice on specific content issues relating to their scientific expertise, either individually or in small working groups

Richard introduced a discussion of the recent rapid increase in submission of manuscripts describing research done by little more than prompts to large language models (LLMs). These often have very little scientific value. Accepting them may benefit their authors but risks reducing the value of the servers as an information source for readers. The challenges presented by AI are complex and the solutions not simple but board members were asked to comment on one proposed policy change: the requirement that corresponding authors have an institutional affiliation so there is an authority to which any issues of research integrity can be referred should they arise. All were in favor.

During the meetings, Board members had questions of their own. The topics included the financial health of openRxiv and plans for sustainability, interest from AI companies in indexing preprints, screening manuscripts describing in-silico research with potential dual use risk, the variability among scientific disciplines in the use of preprints, how to improve on-site search, and approaches to the goal of increasing trust in preprints. There was vocal approval of the partnership with Dryad for data deposition and encouragement to explore relationships with other repositories. There was acknowledgement that LLMs have potential to assist in screening manuscripts. 

### Conclusion
John closed each meeting with a promise to circulate a brief combined summary of both events and to schedule future meetings well in advance to ensure maximum attendance. The double meeting format was appreciated and will be retained. 

These meetings were helpful to the management team who appreciated the Board members’ thoughtful and lively engagement with the issues discussed. They were an excellent beginning to what promises to be a valuable working relationship.
