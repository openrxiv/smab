# openRxiv Scientific Medical and Advisory Board Framework

## 1. About openRxiv
openRxiv is an open-access preprint platform dedicated to accelerating scientific discovery by making research freely available to the global community. We serve researchers, health professionals , policymakers, funders and the public by providing rapid, transparent, and equitable access to scientific findings across disciplines.

The Scientific and Medical Advisory Board (SMAB) is a cornerstone of openRxiv's commitment to scientific rigor, ethical standards, and maintaining community trust. The SMAB provides independent expert guidance to ensure the platform evolves in alignment with the needs of the scientific community and the principles of open science.

## 2. Purpose & Scope
The SMAB exists to:
* Provide independent scientific guidance to the openRxiv leadership team.
* Advise on content policies, moderation frameworks, and scientific integrity standards.
* Represent the interests of the global research community.
* Provide feedback on proposed enhancements to openRxiv’s platform features and tools.
* Support openRxiv's mission of democratizing sharing of and access to scientific knowledge.

## 3. Board Composition
### 3.1 Size & Structure
The SMAB consists of 10–15 members drawn from diverse scientific disciplines, career stages, geographic regions, and professional backgrounds. 

### 3.2 Term Limits
Members serve three-year terms, renewable once for a maximum of six consecutive years. Staggered terms ensure continuity, with subsets of the board rotating.



### 4. Roles & Responsibilities
**4.1 All SMAB Members**
* Participate actively in scheduled meetings and working groups.
* Review and provide written feedback on proposals as needed within agreed timelines.
* Do not disclose confidential information shared in meetings, working groups, convenings, or written communications.
* Serve as ambassadors for openRxiv within their scientific and regional communities.
* Contribute expertise in relevant working groups if available and requested.

***4.2 SMAB Chair***
The Inaugural Chair is selected by the openRxiv Board. The Chair's responsibilities include:
* Setting meeting agendas in collaboration with openRxiv staff.
* Facilitating productive deliberation and ensuring all voices are heard.
* Serving as the primary liaison between the SMAB, openRxiv leadership, and the openRxiv Board.

***4.3 Vice Chair***
The Vice Chair supports the Chair, assumes Chair duties in their absence.

5.2 Meeting Norms
Agendas and discussion documents distributed at least 7 calendar days in advance.
Meeting minutes drafted within 5 business days and approved by the Chair before distribution.
Recordings made available to members only; summaries shared publicly where appropriate.

6. Standing Scientific Working Groups
openRxiv has the ability to establish Scientific Working Groups when needed. Each Scientific Working Group should include at least one SMAB member and at least one openRxiv staff member. Groups must contain at least 4 members and can include members outside of the SMAB. 

Expectations:
The Scientific Working Group will identify a lead who will be required to create and share reports to ensure the effectiveness of the group. 
Group minutes and discussion documents are required to be well documented and recorded.
Each group is required to prepare a report for presentation and discussion at every full Board meeting, delivered by the group leads.
7. Advisory Service & Recognition
Board membership is a voluntary, unpaid role undertaken in service of openRxiv and in support of advancing its mission.
openRxiv publicly acknowledges SMAB members on its website and in its annual report.

8. Conflict of Interest
All SMAB members are required to complete, sign, and return a Conflict of Interest (COI) disclosure form to openRxiv upon appointment. Members must disclose any actual, potential, or perceived conflicts of interest that could influence, or appear to influence, their advice to openRxiv.

When a relevant conflict arises, the member must disclose it to the Chair and appropriate openRxiv staff and, when necessary, recuse themselves from related discussions or decisions. Recusals will be documented as appropriate. Failure to disclose relevant conflicts or to comply with this policy may result in review of the member’s status and potential removal from the SMAB.
9. Onboarding & Offboarding
9.1 Onboarding
New members complete an orientation session within 30 days of appointment.
Orientation covers organization overview, policies, and working groups.


9.2 Offboarding
Departing members participate in a brief exit interview to capture feedback.
Knowledge transfer to successors is facilitated by openRxiv staff.
Alumni may be invited to serve as ad hoc advisors on specific projects.

10. Framework Review & Amendments
This framework is reviewed annually by the SAB and openRxiv leadership. Proposed amendments to this document will be tracked and dated. Material changes are communicated publicly.
